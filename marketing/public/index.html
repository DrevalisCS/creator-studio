What it can do today

Every feature below ships in the current release. Nothing on this list is "coming soon" - see the Roadmap for that.

LLM scripts

LM Studio, Ollama, vLLM, or Claude via API. Series bibles keep voice consistent across episodes. 3-phase chunked generation for long-form.

TTS voiceover

17 Microsoft Edge voices (free), Kokoro, Piper (both local), or ElevenLabs. Per-speaker tags let audiobooks do multiple characters.

Scene generation

ComfyUI workflows - Qwen Image for 9:16 Shorts, Wan 2.2 for 16:9 long-form. Your models, your style. Multi-server pool for parallel generation.

Word-level captions

faster-whisper aligns the TTS audio word-by-word. Burned-in animated ASS subtitles with style presets.

Video assembly

FFmpeg composites scenes + captions + voice + music (sidechain ducking). 9:16, 16:9, 1:1 outputs. Ken Burns pans, transitions.

Audiobook studio

Paste a long text; get a chaptered audiobook with cover image, music, and optional video. Multi-voice casting via bracketed speaker tags.

YouTube upload

OAuth connect, multiple channels, scheduled publishing with retry. SEO metadata + thumbnail picker per episode.

Cloud GPU offload

RunPod integration (Pro tier+). Spin up a ComfyUI pod or a vLLM pod on demand when your local GPU isn't enough.

Background jobs

Generates multiple episodes in parallel, resumes partial failures per-scene, and broadcasts progress via WebSocket.

How a typical workflow looks

Once. Once set up, producing the next episode is 3 clicks.

1

Set up a series (one-time, ~10 min)

Define a series bible (tone, characters, visual style), pick a voice, pick a ComfyUI workflow, pick output format (Shorts / long-form). Reusable across every episode in that series.

2

Give it a topic

Click "Generate episode" and type or paste a topic. The LLM writes the script in your series voice with scene-level visual prompts. Chunked generation for 10+ minute videos keeps coherence across chapters.

3

Review & edit (optional)

Tweak narration, reorder scenes, regenerate individual scene images, swap voices, change caption style - each step is independently re-runnable. Nothing forces you to approve an AI-generated frame before shipping.

4

Let the pipeline run

Six background steps: script - voice - scenes - captions - assembly - thumbnail. Progress is live in the Activity Monitor. Run up to 8 episodes in parallel (hardware permitting).

5

Publish

Upload to YouTube (one click), schedule for later, or export the raw MP4 + thumbnail + description. Studio tier adds TikTok and Instagram.

What it does not do

Being honest upfront is cheaper than refunds. Here is what this software is not:

Not a content-rights clearing service

We generate scripts and imagery via AI models. Whether the output infringes trademarks, likenesses, or copyrights depends on your prompts and the models you load. You are responsible for clearing rights before publishing.

Not a backup service

Your episodes live on your disk. We do not have access to them. Back them up like any other local data. Delete your install and they are gone.

Not a "one click and done" magic machine

Good output needs good prompts, good TTS voices, and reasonable ComfyUI models. The tool saves 90% of the mechanical work. It does not replace editorial judgment - review the AI-generated script before publishing.

Not a substitute for YouTube's own rules

YouTube, TikTok, and Instagram each have their own policies on AI-generated content, disclosure, and authenticity. Follow them. We only hand the file to the upload API; the platform decides.

Not guaranteed stable on exotic setups

We test Docker + Ubuntu 24.04, macOS Docker Desktop, and Windows 11 + WSL2 + Docker Desktop. Arch, BSD, Podman, rootless Docker, corporate-proxied networks - may work, may not. Email support and we will help best-effort.

Not "free forever" if you stop paying

Existing videos keep working. Generation and updates stop 7 days after your subscription lapses. No content is deleted. Re-subscribe and you are back in.

Why self-hosted?

You own the pipeline

Scripts, voices, models, workflows, OAuth tokens - all on your disk. Drevalis has no ability to access, edit, or delete them.

Your GPU, your cost

Pay the license. Compute runs on your hardware. No per-generation fees. Rent a cloud GPU on-demand (Pro tier+) only when you need more.

No vendor lock-in

Swap the LLM, swap the TTS, swap ComfyUI workflows. The app orchestrates open tools; nothing in the pipeline is proprietary to Drevalis.

Questions

What hardware do I need? v

See the hardware matrix. Minimum to generate anything: 16 GB RAM + an NVIDIA GPU with 8 GB VRAM (or RunPod on Pro tier). Long-form video benefits a lot from 16 GB+ VRAM.

Do I need to install ComfyUI and LM Studio separately? v

Yes - both run as external services so you control the models. The install guide walks through it. Alternatively, on Pro tier, point at a RunPod ComfyUI pod and use Claude / OpenAI for scripts - zero local GPU needed.

Can I upgrade or cancel later? v

Yes. Visit drevalis.com/account any time to open Stripe's billing portal - upgrade, downgrade, change payment method, cancel, or view invoices.

What happens if I don't renew? v

7-day grace window where everything keeps running. Then generation and upload lock until you renew. Past episodes remain on disk and playable - nothing is deleted, nothing is transmitted.

Is my data sent anywhere? v

Scripts, voices, videos, and channel credentials stay on your machine. Drevalis servers only handle licensing - a 24h heartbeat checks your subscription. See the Privacy Policy for the short, specific list.

Am I legally allowed to publish AI-generated videos? v

Depends on jurisdiction, platform, and what the video contains. YouTube requires disclosure for altered / synthetic content that might be mistaken for real. TikTok similar. You are responsible for the disclosure and for not infringing copyrights or impersonating real people. See Acceptable Use.

Refund policy? v

14 days, full refund, no questions asked - unless the license has been activated on more than one machine or used to publish more than 10 uploads. See Terms.
