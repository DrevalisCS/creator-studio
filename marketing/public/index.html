What it can do today

Every step of the video pipeline, automated.

Nothing on this page is "coming soon." Every feature below ships in the current release. Upcoming work lives in the Roadmap.

Step 1 · Script

Scripts that stay on-brand across 100 episodes.

Define a series bible once — tone, characters, visual style, intro/outro templates — then feed it any topic. The LLM writes scene-level scripts with narration, visual prompts, and timing, all in your series voice.

  • Local or cloud. LM Studio, Ollama, vLLM, Claude, or OpenAI-compatible — round-robin pool with automatic failover.
  • Long-form aware. 10+ minute videos use a 3-phase chunked generation (outline → chapters → quality pass) that keeps coherence across chapters.
  • Always editable. Rewrite a single scene, regenerate just the hook, swap whole chapters.
Step 2 · Voice

Four TTS engines. Per-character casting. Zero re-uploads.

Mix Microsoft Edge neural voices (free, 300+ available), Kokoro 24 kHz, Piper local ONNX, or ElevenLabs. Bracket speakers in your script and the engine casts them separately — perfect for dialogue-heavy audiobooks.

  • Parallel synthesis. Segments split across multiple ComfyUI servers for long runs.
  • Per-speaker pauses. Context-aware 150 ms / 400 ms gaps between speakers read naturally.
  • Speed + pitch overrides. Tweak without re-rendering the video.
Step 3 · Scenes

Your ComfyUI workflows. Your visual style. Per-scene control.

Qwen Image for 9:16 Shorts, Wan 2.2 for 16:9 long-form — or any ComfyUI workflow you import. Multi-server pool runs scenes in parallel. Need a scene redone? One click, keeps everything else.

  • Visual consistency. Base seed per series + style prompt appended to every scene.
  • Per-scene resumability. Failed mid-run? Completed scenes are kept. Retries resume from the first failure.
  • Cloud GPU (Pro tier). Launch pods on RunPod, Vast.ai, or Lambda Labs straight from the app — pick the cheapest price across providers.
Step 4 · Publish

From render complete to YouTube live in one click.

Multi-channel OAuth, per-channel upload schedules, automatic retries with backoff. Studio tier adds TikTok and Instagram. Or export the raw MP4 plus thumbnail plus description as a bundle.

  • Schedule ahead. Calendar view + per-channel upload days and times, time-zone aware.
  • SEO metadata. Title, description, tags, and chapter markers generated alongside the video.
  • Thumbnail. Auto-generated from a frame of your choice, or upload your own.

Plus the things most creator tools forget.

Small, load-bearing details you only notice when you're producing volume.

Audiobook studio

Long text → chaptered MP3 with ID3 tags, chapter markers, cover, and optional per-chapter images. Multi-voice casting via speaker tags.

Word-level captions

faster-whisper aligns every word to the audio. Burned-in animated subtitles with style presets (YouTube, TikTok, minimal).

Background music

Curated library or AI-generated via AceStep (12 moods). Sidechain ducking under narration. Per-chapter transitions in long-form.

Parallel pipelines

Up to 8 episodes generating simultaneously. Shorts-first / long-form-first / FIFO priority switching.

Full data export

One-click tarball of database + media. Restore is selective — DB only, media only, or both. Swap machines without losing work.

Encrypted secrets at rest

YouTube OAuth tokens, third-party API keys — all Fernet-encrypted with a key that never leaves your install.

Live progress

WebSocket-driven job monitor. Watch every step finish in real time. Resume from the failed step, not from zero.

In-app updates

New release? Settings → Updates → Update now. Updater sidecar handles the pull + restart with live progress.

API access (Studio)

The same REST endpoints the UI uses are available for your own scripts. Bring-your-own API token via the API_AUTH_TOKEN env var.

Central asset library

Upload reference images, B-roll clips, custom music, logos. SHA-256 dedup, tag search, ffprobe metadata. Anywhere you pick an asset, it's the same library.

Video-in pipeline

Drop a raw recording (podcast, webinar, vlog). faster-whisper transcribes it and the LLM picks the best 30–60s moments. One click turns a clip into an episode.

In-browser video editor

Multi-track timeline (video / voice / music / overlays / captions). Trim / split / ripple-delete / drag-reorder. Keyframed text overlays, per-word caption emphasis, drag-draw volume envelope. 480p proxy preview.

Character + style locks

Pin a face across every scene with IPAdapter-FaceID references. Pin a look (lighting, palette, grain) with style-reference assets. Per-scene override if you need it.

Voice cloning

Record a 30-second take in your browser or upload an audio asset. ElevenLabs Instant Voice Cloning creates a profile usable across every series.

SEO pre-flight

Before you upload: traffic-light scoring of title, description, hashtags, thumbnail, opening hook. LLM-generated A/B title and thumbnail variants one click away.

Team mode

Invite collaborators with owner / editor / viewer roles. Opt-in: single-user installs run with no login at all.

Honest first

What Drevalis doesn't do.

Being honest upfront is cheaper than refunds. Here's what this software isn't.

Not a content-rights clearing service

We generate with AI models. Whether the output infringes trademarks or copyrights depends on your prompts and models. You clear rights before publishing.

Not a backup service

Episodes live on your disk — we have no access to them. The built-in tarball backup is yours to store somewhere safe.

Not one-click-and-done magic

Good output needs good prompts, good voices, reasonable ComfyUI models. We save 90% of the mechanical work, not your editorial judgment.

Not a substitute for platform rules

YouTube, TikTok, Instagram each have their own AI-disclosure policies. You follow them — we only hand the file to the upload API.

Not guaranteed stable on exotic setups

Tested: Docker + Ubuntu 24.04, macOS Docker Desktop, Windows 11 + WSL2. Arch, BSD, Podman, corporate proxies — best-effort support.

Not free forever if you cancel

Existing videos keep working. Generation and updates stop 7 days after lapse. No content is deleted. Re-subscribe and you're back in.

You own the pipeline

Scripts, voices, models, workflows, OAuth tokens — all on your disk. Drevalis has no ability to access, edit, or delete them.

Your GPU, your cost

Flat license fee. Compute runs on your hardware. No per-generation billing. Rent a cloud GPU on-demand only when you need it.

No vendor lock-in

Swap LLM, TTS, ComfyUI workflows. The app orchestrates open tools; nothing in the pipeline is proprietary to Drevalis.

Common questions

What hardware do I need?

See the hardware matrix. Minimum to generate anything: 16 GB RAM and an NVIDIA GPU with 8 GB VRAM (or RunPod on Pro tier). Long-form video benefits from 16 GB+ VRAM.

Do I need to install ComfyUI and LM Studio separately?

Yes — both run as external services so you control the models. The install guide walks through it. Alternatively, on Pro tier, point at a RunPod ComfyUI pod and use Claude / OpenAI for scripts — zero local GPU needed.

Can I upgrade or cancel later?

Yes. Visit drevalis.com/account any time to open Stripe's billing portal — upgrade, downgrade, change payment method, cancel, or view invoices.

What happens if I don't renew?

7-day grace window where everything keeps working. Then generation and upload lock until you renew. Past episodes remain on disk and playable — nothing is deleted, nothing is transmitted.

Is my data sent anywhere?

Scripts, voices, videos, and channel credentials stay on your machine. Drevalis servers only handle licensing — a 24 h heartbeat checks your subscription. See the Privacy Policy.

Am I legally allowed to publish AI-generated videos?

Depends on jurisdiction, platform, and content. YouTube and TikTok require disclosure for altered / synthetic content that might be mistaken for real. You're responsible for the disclosure and for not infringing copyrights or impersonating real people. See Acceptable Use.

Refund policy?

14 days, full refund — unless the license has been activated on more than one machine or used to publish more than 10 uploads. See Terms.
